# 📊 Adolescent Birth Rate Analysis (WHO Data) – Short Description (One by One)

### 1. Introduction

Analysis of adolescent birth rates using WHO data to understand teenage pregnancy trends worldwide.

### 2. Problem Statement

High adolescent birth rates can affect maternal health, education, and socioeconomic development.

### 3. Objective

To identify patterns, trends, and regional differences in adolescent birth rates.

### 4. Data Source

Data collected from the World Health Organization database.

### 5. Dataset

Contains adolescent birth rates per 1,000 women aged 15–19 years across countries and years.

### 6. Data Cleaning

Missing values, duplicates, and inconsistencies are removed for accurate analysis.

### 7. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Used to understand data distribution, trends, and important patterns.

### 8. Univariate Analysis

Examines adolescent birth rate statistics such as mean, median, and distribution.

### 9. Bivariate Analysis

Compares birth rates between countries, regions, or years.

### 10. Multivariate Analysis

Studies relationships among multiple variables simultaneously.

### 11. Bar Chart

Shows countries with the highest and lowest adolescent birth rates.

### 12. Line Chart

Displays changes in adolescent birth rates over time.

### 13. Histogram

Shows the frequency distribution of birth rates.

### 14. Box Plot

Identifies outliers and variation in birth rates.

### 15. Pie Chart

Represents regional contributions to adolescent births.

### 16. Heatmap

Visualizes correlations and patterns in the dataset.

### 17. Key Insight 1

Adolescent birth rates vary significantly across countries.

### 18. Key Insight 2

Some regions consistently have higher birth rates than others.

### 19. Key Insight 3

Many countries show a declining trend in adolescent births.

### 20. Key Insight 4

A few countries appear as outliers with exceptionally high rates.

### 21. Findings

Healthcare access and education influence adolescent birth rates.

### 22. Recommendation

Improve reproductive health education and family planning services.

### 23. Impact

Supports policymakers in reducing teenage pregnancies.

### 24. Conclusion

The analysis helps understand global adolescent pregnancy trends and supports better public health decisions.
